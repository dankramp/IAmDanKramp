Young Life has changed my life.

I have always been a part of Young Life.

Growing up in a Christian home, it has always been the imperative of my parents to sustain my involvement in church-based activities. Before you get the wrong impression, I don't mean to say that they deprived me of fun, "normal kid" activites; in fact, quite the opposite is true. My parents raised myself and my two brothers in such a way that faith was as much a personal decision as it was appropriate for a child to decide on his own. They had always sought to discover the most exciting and engaging means by which we should pursue our faith, given our lack of attentiveness and need for constant entertainment.

Therefore throughout my childhood, I was involved in many Christian organizations - VBS, summer camps, events hosted weekly by my church - I for the fun of it, my parents for the benefit of my faith. Of course I had an appreciation for the spiritual side of it all - my parents modeled this well for me and made sure to instill proper morality in me. However, it never interested me enough that I should learn more on my own or truly seek to discover who this God really is.

As a result, my friends and I treated all of these events as opportunities to cause trouble together and see how much we could get away with. And I will say, we realized our limits many times over the years. I wish I could write of all the times we had been pulled aside and lectured at church events, or warned of the imminent phone call to our parents, or even the time that we were asked not to return to a Christian summer camp in 8th grade (we had gone solely with the intention of causing trouble) - but I fear I might never conclude this article. And so, as high school appeared on the horizon, I looked eagerly towards the pinnacle of fun in the realm of Christianity - Young Life.

Young Life is an organization dedicated to "introducing adolescents to Jesus Christ and helping them grow in their faith," according to their mission statement. Volunteer leaders who are college aged or older and staff people are assigned to a high school to meet kids and become involved in their lives. They put on weekly meetings for students called Club and Campaigners. Club is a night of excitement - silly games, loud songs, hilarious skits, and a conclusion with a story from the Bible - while Campaigners is time set aside for more in depth studies of the Word.

Young Life was held in high regard by my older brothers who had the distinct privilege of being in high school and, therefore, the sole qualification for attending. Though I had been in Wyld Life, the middle school equivalent, I knew that Young Life was leagues ahead in terms of craziness and enjoyment. And so for three years - through my Junior year - that was what it was: just another, though superior, fun Christian activity.
